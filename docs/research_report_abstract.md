# Abstract

Mental health challenges are increasingly prevalent worldwide, with approximately 970 million people affected, yet traditional therapeutic interventions remain inaccessible to many due to cost, stigma, and limited professional resources. This research addresses this gap through the development of Reflectly, an intelligent journaling application that leverages advanced artificial intelligence techniques to provide personalized emotional guidance at scale. We conceptualize emotional wellbeing as a navigation problem and implement a novel graph-based approach where emotional states are represented as nodes, transitions as weighted edges, and optimal paths are identified using A* search algorithms. 

Our methodology integrates emotion recognition, A* pathfinding, probabilistic reasoning using Markov models and Bayesian networks, and an intelligent agent architecture, all supported by a scalable big data infrastructure combining Kafka, HDFS, and Spark. We evaluate this approach using the IEMOCAP dataset and anonymized mental health data, implementing a multi-faceted assessment framework that examines both technical performance and psychological effectiveness.

The results demonstrate that the A* pathfinding algorithm successfully identifies optimal emotional transition paths, with the system effectively balancing path length with psychological appropriateness. Testing revealed successful emotion recognition functionality, appropriate path generation, and effective transition recommendations, though with certain limitations in handling non-standard emotions and providing highly personalized recommendations for new users. Comparative analysis indicates significant advantages over traditional journaling applications and existing emotional wellbeing tools, particularly in personalization capabilities and theoretical grounding.

This research makes several key contributions: (1) a novel emotional pathfinding framework that formalizes emotional regulation as finding optimal paths through an emotional state space; (2) a successful implementation of A* search for emotional guidance; (3) probabilistic models of emotional dynamics; and (4) a scalable big data architecture for emotional analytics. Future directions include enhanced emotion recognition, multimodal inputs, advanced causal modeling, and applications in clinical support and preventive mental health. Ultimately, Reflectly represents a significant advancement in digital mental health, demonstrating how computational techniques can be effectively applied to emotional wellbeing support at scale.
