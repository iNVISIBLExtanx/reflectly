# Abstract

Mental health challenges affect nearly one billion people globally, yet traditional therapeutic interventions remain inaccessible to many. This research addresses this gap through Reflectly, an intelligent journaling application that provides personalized emotional guidance using advanced AI techniques. We conceptualize emotional wellbeing as a navigation problem, implementing a graph-based approach where emotional states are nodes, transitions are weighted edges, and optimal paths are identified using A* search algorithms.

Our methodology integrates emotion recognition, A* pathfinding, probabilistic reasoning with Markov models and Bayesian networks, and an intelligent agent architecture, all supported by a scalable big data infrastructure (Kafka, HDFS, Spark). We utilize the IEMOCAP dataset and anonymized mental health data for evaluation, examining both technical performance and psychological effectiveness.

Results demonstrate successful identification of optimal emotional transition paths, balancing path length with psychological appropriateness. Testing confirmed effective emotion recognition, path generation, and transition recommendations, with limitations in handling non-standard emotions and new-user personalization. Comparative analysis shows advantages over traditional journaling applications in personalization capabilities and theoretical grounding.

Key contributions include: (1) a novel emotional pathfinding framework that formalizes emotional regulation as finding optimal paths through an emotional state space; (2) successful implementation of A* search for emotional guidance; (3) probabilistic models of emotional dynamics; and (4) a scalable big data architecture for emotional analytics. Future directions include enhanced emotion recognition, multimodal inputs, and applications in clinical support and preventive mental health. Reflectly demonstrates how computational techniques can effectively support emotional wellbeing at scale, addressing critical gaps in global mental health resources.
